Terms


  • Sections (by FIDE Rating)
    There will be three sections: Master (>1800), Major (1100 -1900), and Challenger (<1200 and unrated). If you have a FIDE rating, you will use that to determine which section you should enter. If you do not have a FIDE rating, you will use your national federation rating. If you don't have a FIDE or national federation rating, you are unrated and so will enter the Challenger section.

    The Tournament Director reserves the right to refuse entry or to transfer players between sections without giving a reason. There will be live boards for the Master section games that will broadcast those games online.

    All sections will be FIDE rated.

    The Tournament Director reserves the right to merge or split sections based on the number of registrations.

  • Round Opponent
    The pairings for all sections for the first round will be published on the tournament website on the evening of Thursday, April 28th. Late entrants will be paired against each other where possible. The next round matches will be displayed as soon as they are available online and at the tournament administration area.

  • Playing Schedule
    Round 1 Fri April 29th 7:00pm
    Round 2 Sat April 30th 11:00am
    Round 3 Sat April 30th 4:00pm
    Round 4 Sun May 1st 10:00am
    Round 5 Sun May 1st 3:00pm

  • Playing Area
    No food and no chewing gum in the playing area.

  • Equipment
    All boards, pieces, clocks, and score sheets will be provided by the tournament.

  • Registration
    Click on the Registration link at the top of the webpage or here to go to event registration page. The cost of early registration is €45. Early registration will end on Friday, April 1st at which point the late registration fee is €50. Registrations after the round 1 pairings have been posted on Thursday, April 28th, will require an additional €10. Registrations on the first day of the tournament will require an additional €20. Acceptance of entries is entirely at the discretion of the Tournament Director.

  • Time Control
    All moves to be completed in 90 minutes, plus a 30 second increment after each move. All moves must be notated on provided scoresheets. Any player arriving more than 30 minutes late will forfeit their match.

  • Byes
    A single Half-point bye is permitted in any one of the first four rounds. Pairings will be effected using the FIDE approved program and results will be published on the appropriate websites. Players may request a "Travelling Bye". All communication to the Tournament Director must be prior to 2 hours before the first game. After round 1 all "Travelling Bye" requests should be made to the Arbiter before the end of the previous round.

  • Prize money
    Master: 1st €600, 2nd €300, 3rd €175, 4th €125
    Major: 1st €350, 2nd €175, 3rd €100, 4th €75
    Challenger: 1st €350, 2nd €175, 3rd €100, 4th €75

    If ties occur, prize money will be shared equally. The prizes are subject to change at the Tournament Director's discretion. The prizes will be distributed at the awards ceremony at the conclusion of the tournament on Sunday evening at 7pm.

  • Chess Rules
    FIDE Laws of Chess Apply as per FIDE Handbook. Tiebreak criteria: Direct Encounter, Median Buchholz; Buchholz;Progressive Score. If players cannot be separated after this, the title shall be shared. In the event of any dispute during the game, the players should stop the clock and look for an Arbiter to rule on the issue. The decision of the Arbiter shall be final.

    At the end of each game, players must set the board up correctly (i.e. White Rook on a1). Sets and clocks must not be removed from the playing room. Clocks must be left at the side of the board where placed by the Arbiter.

  • Social Events
    There will be a gathering for players at a local venue on Saturday evening 7pm-12:30am. There will be one free drink for every player as well as a reduced price menu. There might also be an informal Sunday evening blitz tournament following the awards ceremony at a location TBD.

  • Hotels
    Click the link at the top of the page or here for information on local hotels and the associated tournament discounts.

  • Covid
    We will be taking precautions to prevent the spread of COVID-19 in line with government recommendations at the time of the tournament. If you are unwell, please do not attend the event. There will be hand sanitizer available for all players. All equipment will be wiped clean between rounds. Players who have finished their games shall be considered spectators and should leave the playing area.

  • Cheating
    Cheating will result in expulsion from the tournament. Once the game has started, no player shall consult with books, electronic devices or any other player, except the Arbiter until after the game has finished. No player will be allowed to have on their person any electronic device, including a phone, during a match.There will be a cloak room to check electronic devices during matches. Refunds will not be issued to expelled players.

  • Disclaimer
    Players accept as a condition of entry that the tournament director's and arbiter's decision on any aspect of the tournament is final, and that the information provided on the entry form may be recorded and used for administration, mailing lists, and grading purposes. Photos could be taken for use on the Sligo Chess Club website, social media, or the FIDE website. If you don't want your photo published, please inform the tournament director. Acts of God or other reasons beyond the tournament director's control may necessitate the cancellation of the tournament. The tournament director will refund as much of the entry fee as possible, but no guarantees are made. Mobile phones and other portable communication devices must be muted during rounds. Players are not permitted to bring mobile phones or other electronic devices into the playing area. If a player is found to have a phone or other electronic devices once the round has started, that player will forfeit that round's match. The tournament director accepts no responsibility for any damage, theft, or accident to the person or property of anyone attending this event. Parents or guardians are responsible for their children during the tournament. The organisers and helpers are not able to act "in loco parentis" and are not able to take responsibility for any child's actions, or for the actions of anyone that may affect your child.
